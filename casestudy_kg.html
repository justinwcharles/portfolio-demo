Brief

Our mission for this project was to find a local client who was in need of an online shop to sell items for their business. We wanted to focus on responsive web design with a mobile-first approach.

Our client was Kelli Golden-Powell of KG Creates. Kelli crafts and sells beautiful earrings with unique designs made of clay and brass.

Problem

Shoppers love the convenience of shopping online and supporting local businesses. However, they struggle to make purchases without being able to experience the weight, texture and quality of jewelry.

How might we reduce the risk of buying products online to allow shoppers to feel confident in making online purchases?

Solution

Inspire confidence in shoppers with quality product images, reviews, and advertising KG‚Äôs pop-up shops to invite customers to shop in person. Keep visitors engaged with a straightforward navigating experience, a simple checkout flow, and details about how the product is made and where materials are sourced from.

Discovery & Definition

We began with a kickoff meeting with the client to understand more about her product, preferences, and branding. A survey was conducted which garnered responses from 53 women between 16-35 years old. We then conducted 6 semi-structured interviews with women between the ages of 23-34 who regularly wear jewelry and shop online.

From this research, we gained some key insights:

  • Although 85% of survey respondents value quality most, they struggle to perceive the quality of items when shopping online
  • People feel engaged with a brand when they understand the ‚Äúwhy‚Äù and ‚Äúhow‚Äù behind product creation
  • A swift, secure checkout experience with diverse payment options and stored information is critical

After becoming familiar with the user, we defined the key features. First, we used our research to define a list of features. Then, we plotted them on a feature prioritization matrix to understand what we should tackle first to be able to deliver a minimum viable product.

Then, we created a sitemap to plan the information architecture for the site. Our approach was to keep the amount of pages to a minimum so we did not distract the user from the primary purpose of the site: to make a purchase.

Lo-Fi Design

To start the visual design process, we had 2 group members quickly sketch out some page iterations to quickly get some ideas out. This was our jumping off point to compare and discuss some different ideas.

We then created some wireframes and began user testing, focusing on the shop and checkout flow since this was the primary purpose of the site. We focused our testing on mobile since we believe the majority of users will be experiencing the shop from this size device.

Our test plan was simple: ask users to purchase a pair earrings.

This required users to land on the home page, navigate to the shop and then to the product page, add earrings to their cart, and go through the checkout flow.

Going from home to cart yielded flawless execution from all users we tested with.

The checkout flow is where we learned some key insights

  • There were too many screens to navigate through
  • Even though there was a progress tracker, navigating back and forth through the flow was confusing
  • The naming of the buttons didn't make it clear that you were moving to the next step

To alleviate all of these concerns, we decided to move to a 1 page checkout experience.

Hi-Fi Design

With our lo-fi design and testing complete, it was time to define the visual style and move into hi-fi. Based on our kickoff meeting with the client, we knew we wanted the site to have a tone that was:

  • personable,
  • warm,
  • and inviting.

The client was inspired by a desert sunset theme which was also reflected in her earring designs. With these principles in mind we created a mood board she was very happy with.

Using the mood board as inspiration, we created the following style guide. It went through several iterations throughout the design process, and we and our client were pleased with the final outcome.



The style guide was utilized to transform the lo-fi mockups into a near-finished product. Group members were assigned different parts of the site to work on, and then I reviewed and updated each page on my own to ensure a consistent look and feel through the site.

Finally, we put the finishing touches on the design and programmed working prototypes for mobile and desktop

spective

After the project was completed, we presented the final product to Kelli. She was thrilled with our work and expressed interest in working with a developer to turn our design into a functioning website once she prepares her business to be able to take on online orders and shipping. We look forward to working through that process and seeing our vision come to life.

Working through this project has reinforced the importance of communication and collaboration when working with a client and a team of designers

At the beginning of this project we made sure to understand the requirements and preferences of our client and to align on a way of working as a design team, which set us up for success to work efficiently through the design process with a limited amount of time.
